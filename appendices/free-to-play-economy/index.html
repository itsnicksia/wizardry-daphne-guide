    Free-to-Play Resources

    Certain resources in Wizardry Variants Daphne can be used to obtain in-game content or upgrade your adventuring party. While many can be purchased at the Wizardry Variants Daphne Official Web Shop, Wizardry Variants Daphne Web Store, and at the Jeweler (see Guide Purchase Evaluaions page), Free-to-Play (F2P) players rely on in-game resource accumulation over time. The summary below tries to cover regular, reliable resource accumulation methods. Intermittent events can provide additional resource gain opportunities, some of which are noted where appropriate.

    TL,DR

    F2P players should not expect to get every banner with only free resources. You'll have to pick and choose. There's no ad-view gem farm. And no game progress is blocked or difficulty gated requiring non-free resources. This is not that kind of gacha. Just enjoy the game.

    Gems

    Two Gem types: Gems of Erin (Purple) and Gems of Org (Green).
    Purple Gems must be purchased through one of the methods mentioned above. Green gems can be purchased as well, but can also be obtained in-game for free. Both gem types generally exchange at the same rate for items, although there are certain Purple-gem-only Jeweler items. Unless noted otherwise, details below refer only to Green Gems.

    Uses

    Gems can be used in the game as a general currency for upgrades at the Jeweler (adventurer's remains, equipment packs, spellbooks, class change books, etc.) and in-game activities (resurrections, Mausoleum assistance, expanded inventory space, relic removal, etc.). Some in-game activities let you use gems instead of Gold for the same item. Gems are scarce enough that use for those cases is discouraged. Popular uses are listed here:

    "Banner" Adventurer Remains:

    • The most popular gem use is for Limited time special adventurer's remains that give a chance (typically 2%) to get new Legendary characters or new skins for existing characters.
    • These special remains typically cost 200 gems each from the Jeweler, or 11 remains for 2,000 gems.
    • Each Limited Remains usually gives, along with the random character, one 'pity' token that can be exchanged at the Jeweler for remains, equipment, and supplies.
    • Guaranteed banner character remains typically cost 100 (for legendary) or 60 (for non-legendary) tokens.
    • At a 2% success rate, the probability of at least one success in N pulls is: P(N) = 1 - 0.98^N. So odds of having pulled one character on the way to reaching pity is: P(60)=70.2%, P(100)=86.7%
    • So odds are fairly good you will get the banner character before hitting the 'pity' limit, but the gem-cost of a 100% guarantee banner pull is:
      • 60: 5 x 2,000 + 5 x 200 = 11,000 gems
      • 100: 9 x 2,000 + 1 x 200 = 18,200 gems
      • Banner events sometimes provide 2-4 free remains that slightly reduce the above "guarantee" amount by 200-600 gems.
      • The maximum regular gem rate of 3,300 gems/month gives enough for '100% pull' on legendary banners every 18,200 / 3,300 = 5.5 months.
      • Usually this is significantly reduced by irregular sources. Monthly 'possible' gem rates above of 3,900-5,850 reduce this to 3.1-4.7 months.
      • Banner adventurers generally come out more often than this. Even with other Irregular Gem Sources F2P players should not expect to get every banner with free resources.

    Handbooks

    • Ally class changes require class-change handbooks or similar items for every class change event for that character.
    • The Jeweler offers four random class change books each week at a cost of 400 gems each (two are limited to Purple Gems).

    Inventory Storage Increase:

    • The default inventory storage space is 500 slots.
    • The limit can be increased by 25 items for 100 gems up to a new total of 1,000 slots for total of 2000 gems.

    Adventurer's Guild Adventurer Limit Increase:

    • The default registered adventurer headcount limit is 100.
    • The limit can be increased by 10 slots for 100 gems up to new total of 200 slots for a total of 1,000 gems.

    Adventurer's Remains

    Uses:

    In this game, Adventurer's Remains are used by the Masked Adventurer to resurrect allies to help him in his adventure. Remains can be specific to one particular adventurer, or to a group or type of adventurer, or can be ordinary remains that pull randomly from the entire adventurer pool. Gaining additional adventurers increases your party options and gaining duplicate copies of adventurers can increase the power of your adventuring party in numerous ways by allowing skills to be inherited or increased in power, or by providing adventurer tags for grading up to higher levels. As such accumulating adventurer remains is one of the primary ways, even a necessary way, of advancing in the game.

    Guild Tags

    Guild Tags are required to increase a character's Guild Grade that sets the maximum level the adventurer can reach. An exam must be passed and a Guild Tag payment must be made to rise above level 20, and for every 10 levels thereafter. In addition, the required Guild Tag is formed by Unifying two tags of the previous level.

    Tag Requirements per Adventurer per Grade

    Adventurer Grade Max Level Tag required Ungraded Tag Count Cumulative Ungraded Tags Party of 6 Cumulative Tags
    Ungraded 20 n/a n/a 0 0
    Lead 30 Ungraded 1 1 6
    Bronze 40 Lead 2 3 18
    Iron 50 Bronze 4 7 42
    Steel 60 Iron 8 15 90
    Copper 70 Steel 16 31 186

    As this table shows. the required number of tags for each grade grows quickly and tag accumulation can become a primary bottleneck for advancing in the games. They can be accumulated from some of the following activities.

    Optional Guild Tags

    Guild tags can be obtained by dismissing (retiring) adventurers from the guild. See full details in the FAQ.

    Ungraded Adventurer Type Tags received on dismissal
    Anonymous 1 Ungraded
    General 2 Ungraded
    Legendary 1 Iron Grade, 1 Ungraded (9 Ungraded equivalent)

    At high grades, dismissing extra adventurers may be necessary or collecting enough Guild Tags to advance. Using the numbers of regularly available adventurer remains as described above, the available tags are:

    Remains Tags from dismissing 39 Ungraded Remains
    All Anonymous 39
    All General 78
    All Legendary 39 ungraded, 39 iron
    Expected Value saving legendaries 54.6 ungraded
    Expected Value using legendaries 56.55 ungraded, 1.95 iron

    using the standard adventurer remains drop rates: Anon:50%, General:45%, Legend:5%.

    From this we see almost 75 ungraded tags can be obtained monthly using regular means (direct tags + dismissing all regular remains). Including legendary dismissals, the iron tags make it equivalent to about 92 ungraded tags, noting that iron tags cannot be 'downgraded' and are only useful to reach Steel and higher grades.

    While it does involve a player deliberately sacrificing other party improvement options for tags, we can consider including the regular 3,300 Gems/month to produce ~18 additional remains to increase the total available for dismissal to about 57:

    Remains Tags from dismissing 57 Ungraded Remains
    All Anonymous 57
    All General 114
    All Legendary 57 ungraded, 57 iron
    Expected Value saving legendaries 79.8 ungraded
    Expected Value using legendaries 82.65 ungraded, 2.85 iron

    using the standard adventurer remains drop rates: Anon:50%, General:45%, Legend:5%.

    Thus, the maximum ungraded tag rate using direct means plus all regular gems to get and dismiss remains is about 101 tags per month if don't include dismissing legendary adventurers, and 127 if you do.

    Time requried to grade up your party

    Using these numbers, the time to grade up a 6 member adventuring party:

    Regular Tags alone dismissing regular Anon/General Adventurers and Legendary Adventurers including Gem bought Anon/Gen Adventurers and Legendary Adventurers
    6 member party ungraded-to-iron tag accum. time 2.1 months 2.4 weeks 2.4 weeks 1.8 weeks 1.8 weeks
    6 member party iron-to-steel accum. time 2.4 months 2.8 weeks 2.3 weeks 2.1 weeks 1.7 weeks
    6 member party ungraded-to-steel tag accum. time 4.5 months 5.2 weeks 4.2 weeks 3.9 weeks 3.1 weeks
    6 member party steel-to-copper tag accum. time 4.8 months 5.6 weeks 4.5 weeks 4.2 weeks 3.3 weeks
    6 member party ungraded-to-copper tag accum. time 9.3 months 10.8 weeks 8.7 weeks 8.0 weeks 6.4 weeks

    All told, the tag economy does place a throttle on free-to-play adventurer progress. A player with game knowledge could race through the first few abysses in a few days, but without financial investment they will find themselves level-locked by the free-to-play Guild Tag economy until they accumulate enough for advancement.
