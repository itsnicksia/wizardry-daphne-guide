### Basics 

??? "Mechanics" 

    - Each Abyss has a small number of items from chests or Guild requests that will respawn after a certain amount of time has passed. The game uses a hidden cooldown timer and when it reaches zero ("cd = 0") the item will respawn and can be collected again.
    - The cooldown timer starts when a chest is opened. The game does not use a universal daily, weekly, or monthly cooldown system outside a handful of items listed under Exceptions. 
    
??? "Respawn Intervals" 

    - The majority of items are monthly [30 days] and have a chance to respawn starting at Day 21 with an increasing % chance on each subsequent day up to Day 30. After 30 days an item has a 100% chance to appear.
    - For weekly [7 days] items they will respawn after 7 days, but can take up to 9 days in rare cases.
    - It is recommended to pick a day each month to collect your targeted items. This is commonly done with the Abyss bones and major story reward items. The alternative is to check on a daily basis, which can become time-intensive.

### How to Respawn Items

!!! warning " Forced Respawning"

    === "Definition"
        - The game is inconsistent with respawning items on its own after the cd = 0. Therefore, you will need to do a "forced respawn" to make them appear using the Cursed Wheel. 
        - The Cursed Wheel node "Fresh Start" (The Beginning Abyss) functions as an in-game, univeral reset button to respawn chests, fix broken event flags, or resolve minor bugs.
        - Know upfront that Cursed Wheeling outside of your current Abyss will reset all your maps and may re-roll a different floor variation when you return. This is less of an issue now with the upgrades to the auto-pathing system. 

    === "Issues with Abyss 2"

        - Many players have encountered difficulties with respawning items in Abyss 2 (Port Town Grand Legion) due to how the event flags are programmed. 
        - Forced respawning only works when the Cursed Wheel toggles are set to the timeline and ending where Melgina is defeated. We suspect that the event flags that trigger Octoranus's appearance effectively turn off or disable the cooldown timers. We have limited information on the exact mechanical reason why this happens. 
        - This is why Abyss 2 has its own sub-section as it requires a few additional steps. 

=== "Troubleshooting"

        - If you have followed the steps and nothing is working, then it is likely one of the following from most to least likely:
            - The cooldown timer has not yet reached zero ("0"). If you are not using the Respawn Tracker tool or recording the dates in time in Excel, Notepad, etc. then it is likely you may be overestimating the amount of time that has passed since you last opened the chest. Wait a few more days and try again. 
            - The steps need to be completed exactly as written.
            - A recent maintenance update has reset the cooldown timers or disabled them. 
            - Your game is bugged and you may want to consider sending an Inquiry to Drecom so it can be investigated. This would be in an extreme situation where no items have respawned after 30 to 60 days. 

??? note "Map Chests Steps" 

    === "Steps" 
        - Go to the Ruins and select the Cursed Wheel.
        - Using the arrows at the top of the screen toggle left to Abyss 1 (The Beginning Abyss). Select the "Fresh Start" node and then "Leap".
        - The fastest dialogue options are "No problem" and "Do nothing".
        - Return to the Ruins and Leap to your furthest node.
        - Go the appropriate Abyss and collect your items.

    === "Technical Notes" 
        - The steps should work consistently for Abysses 1, 3 and 4. 
        - For Abyss 1 to get the platforms to re-appear on Floor 7 you will need to wheel twice on "King's Arrival" or the final node of your most recently cleared Abyss (i.e., the GWO has been defeated). You can also Harken to Floor 8 and reverse the two closest stones.  
        - The Catacombs is bugged. The chest that contains the monthly bone will not appear on your mini-map. That means you have to go through the entire floor, do the candle puzzle, and check to see if the chest respawned. 

??? note "Abyss 2 Map Chests Steps"

    === "Steps" 

        - Go to the Ruins and select the Cursed Wheel.
        - Use the arrows at the top of the screen to toggle left or right to Port Town Grand Legion.
        - Scroll down to "Return to the Capital" node and toggle (yellow cross-roads icon) "Melgina Defeated". 
        - Do NOT "Leap" yet. This is important.
        - Go back to the top of the screen and toggle left to The Beginning Abyss. 
        - Select "Fresh Start" and "Leap". 
        - The fastest dialogue options are "No problem" and "Do nothing".
        - Return to the Ruins and toggle back to Port Town Grand Legion. 
        - Scroll down to "Rickert's Shop" and "Leap". 
        - If you want the Mana Elixir x3 that is located in the Treasure Room via the deck hatch you will need to select "Arena 5". 
        - Use the nearby Harken and go to your desired floor. 

    === "Technical Notes" 

        - Bounties
            - If you have a high Bounty Rank make sure you toggle back to "Octoranus Defeated" or the Rank 8 and above bounties may not appear. 
        - Pier (District 7) Bone 
            - If the listed steps do not work, then there is an alternative method that is slightly longer. 
            - Follow all of the steps listed previously, but instead of "Leaping" to "Rickert's Shop" you will need to select "One-Eyed Sahuagin" and manually go through District 6 again to reach the Pier. 
            - This is likely due to an event flag being stuck and the only way to reset it is to leap to a point _before_ you encounter the chest for the first time.  
        - Last Resort Method
            - If nothing you are doing is working, then you can toggle to The Beginning Abyss and "Leap" to "Awakening". 
            - You will have to go through the entire tutorial area again, which can take 10 to 15 minutes. 

??? note "Important Requests Steps"

    === "Steps" 
        - These steps are for the the Important Request faction rewards for defeating a Greater Warped One ("GWO"). 
        - Go to the Ruins and select the Cursed Wheel.
        - Use the arrows at the top of the screen to toggle left or right to your desired Abyss.
        - The second node has a toggle (yellow cross-roads icon) called "Client" that lets you select your faction. Note that you must have first defeated the GWO with that faction for this process to work.
        - Select your desired faction. Do NOT Leap at this point. This is important.
        - Scroll down to the second-to-last node, which is always the one directly before you fight the GWO.
            - Abyss 2 - Arena Round 5
            - Abyss 3 - Fortress 3rd Floor Arrival
            - Abyss 4 - Truth of the Beautiful Ore     
        - "Leap" to this node.
        - Go through the required steps to reach and defeat the GWO. 

    === "Ending Requirements"
        - Abyss 3 and Abyss 4 both have "bad" endings where you fail and the Important Request is not fulfilled. 
        - In the Cursed Wheel you will need to set the other toggles to ensure the conditions are correct for the minimum requirements to complete the Important Request. 
        - Note that it does not matter if it is the "Good" or "True" ending as the reward is the same. Do the "Good" ending since the boss fight will be easier and take less time. 

    === "Abyss 3 Cursed Wheel Settings"
        - You need an overall Abyss corrosion level of 8 for the Blackmail Ending ("GWO 8").
        - Corrosion Sentry Toggles
            - Assuming you have all 13 Corrosion Sentry toggles set 8 of them to "Spotted". 
            - Any sentries that you have not registered are automatically counted as "Spotted."

    === "Abyss 4 Cursed Wheel Settings"
        - Clearing GWO 4-2 is the minimum requirement to fulfill the Important Request. 
        - Below are the Cursed Wheel nodes and toggle settings for that version of the fight.
        - Short Rest in the Cabin
            - North Lakeshore Cabin - Fixed it up
            - Dwarves' Cave - Does not matter
            - Village Adults - Did not encounter them 
        - Being That Dwells in the House of God
            - Iris's Eyes - Eyesight was not restored 

### Cooldown Formula 

- We do not know the exact cooldown formula that Drecom uses. Wowever we do have several months of user's data that allows us to make some approximations.
- Minimum Number of Days
    - Items can respawn as early as 21 Days. No item in the dataset has ever spawned before that point.
    - 21 Days functions as a fixed value in the formula.
- "Yes or No" Respawn Roll
    - After the minimum number of days (21 Days) condition has been met the game will make a binary roll - "Yes" or "No" - that determines whether the item will respawn or not.
    - The data suggests that the "Yes" % chance starts at a low value at Day 21 and increases after each roll until it reaches 100% on Day 30.
    - The time interval between each "Yes or No" roll is likely 12 to 24 hours.
- Game Maintenance
    - It is not uncommon for chests to respawn immediately after a maintenance update. This has become less frequent over time.
    - We suspect that it is because the maintenance updates forces all (or some) cooldown timers to zero ("0") in order for the new code, bug fixes, etc. to work properly.      

### Exceptions

There are some items in the game that have a fixed cooldown period, including:

- Abyss 1, Floor 8 - Nourishing Draught x3 (SP pot) - every 24 hours. 
- Bonepicker Weekly Bone and Tallow - every 7 days (same as the Ancient Mausoleum Tallow whose cooldown timer is visible).
- Enemy Weekly Chest Bone - every 7 days. Note that it will overwrite whatever was previously in that chest, including relics, rare enemy drops, or boss items.
